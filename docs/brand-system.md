# STUNN Brand System

STUNN is a premium modern coffee ritual brand. It is not a supplement brand with coffee flavouring, and it should not behave like a biohacker startup. The product promise is emotional first: everything people love about coffee, without the part that makes them feel worse.

## Positioning

STUNN sells the coffee ritual rebuilt for people who want calm ambition. The product should feel like a considered daily object: useful, quiet, premium, and emotionally intelligent.

The core message:

> Everything you love about coffee. None of what you don't.

Supporting ideas:

- Coffee without consequences.
- Calm focus, steady energy, no jitters, no crash.
- A ritual that supports modern work without overstimulation.
- A cup for people who still want performance, but no longer want chaos.

## Brand Feeling

STUNN should feel:

- Calm ambition
- Editorial
- Restrained
- Premium
- Cinematic
- Emotionally intelligent
- Modern luxury wellness
- Quietly productive
- Confident without shouting

STUNN should not feel:

- Biohacker startup
- Generic supplement brand
- SaaS UI
- Over-designed Tailwind
- Cheap DTC
- Influencer wellness
- Clinical nootropics
- Gym-performance powder

## Reference World

Primary references:

- Aesop: restraint, space, sensory language, quiet confidence.
- Le Labo: materiality, ritual, product-as-object.
- Apple wellness: clarity, hierarchy, calm interaction.
- Soho House: hospitality, warmth, adult taste.
- Premium Japanese cafe design: negative space, craft, quiet detail.
- Luxury hospitality editorial: image-led rhythm, fewer claims, more atmosphere.

These references should guide proportion, pacing, copy density, and material feeling. They should not be copied literally.

## Color System

Current implementation tokens:

- STUNN purple: `#5A3493`
- Light lavender body background: `#EDEAEF`
- Light purple surface: `#EDE9F8` / `#EEEAF8`
- Pink accent: `#F9CEE1`
- Cream: `#fef8dd`
- Sage: `#C1D0A5`
- Star gold: `#EFAF00`
- Text: gray/near-black for body, purple for brand-led headings

Usage rules:

- Purple is the brand anchor. Use it for primary identity, strong CTAs, table emphasis, footer/nav, and key headings.
- Light lavender should carry calm page backgrounds and product ritual sections.
- Cream should be used sparingly for warmth, guarantees, light editorial blocks, or final CTA contrast.
- Pink is a secondary accent, not a general UI color.
- Sage should be used carefully. It can suggest calm/natural but quickly makes the brand feel supplement-like if overused.
- Avoid adding new random colors. If a new color is needed, derive it from the existing palette and document why.

## Typography

Current implementation:

- Headings: Anton via `--font-anton`
- Body: Inter via `--font-inter`

Rules:

- Anton works best in uppercase, short, declarative lines.
- Do not use Anton for long claims, paragraphs, or dense labels.
- Inter should carry product explanation, UI labels, legal copy, price detail, and supporting body.
- Premium hierarchy should come from scale, whitespace, and contrast, not decorative effects.
- Avoid cramped all-caps tracking in small UI unless the label is genuinely functional.

Typography direction:

- Hero headlines should be large but not frantic.
- Editorial headings should breathe. Use fewer words.
- Body copy should feel like hospitality, not supplement education.
- Ingredient language should be clear but secondary to ritual and feeling.

## Imagery

Packaging rules:

- Never alter the STUNN packaging.
- Preserve packaging accuracy in every image and layout.
- Do not recolor boxes, sachets, labels, or core pack artwork.

Image direction:

- Product imagery should feel tactile, real, and premium.
- Lifestyle imagery should show calm work, coffee ritual, and quiet focus.
- Avoid generic supplement powder, gym-desk, neon nootropic, or fake tech-product imagery.
- Prefer fewer, stronger images over many small thumbnails.
- Product gallery images must be expandable when used as purchase evidence.

## Voice

The voice is calm, adult, precise, and emotionally aware.

Use:

- "ritual"
- "calm focus"
- "steady energy"
- "without the jitters"
- "without the crash"
- "coffee, without the consequences"
- "everything you love about coffee"

Avoid:

- "hack"
- "supercharge"
- "limitless"
- "biohack"
- "stack"
- "crush your day"
- over-medical claims
- ingredient-first sales language above the fold

## Core Tension

The brand lives in the tension between ambition and restraint. STUNN is for people who still want to perform, but no longer want to be overstimulated.

This should shape every major section:

- Sell the state first.
- Explain the mechanism second.
- Use proof to support, not dominate.
