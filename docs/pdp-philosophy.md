# STUNN PDP Philosophy

The STUNN product page should sell feeling before formula.

STUNN is coffee ritual, not supplement education. The PDP should make the customer want the state: calm focus, steady ambition, better sleep, and the identity of being someone who still performs without being overstimulated.

## Core Message

> Everything you love about coffee. None of what you don't.

Supporting PDP language:

- Coffee, without the consequences.
- Calm focus without caffeine.
- Keep the ritual. Lose the jitters.
- Steady energy without the crash.
- Your coffee ritual, upgraded.

## What the PDP Should Sell

The PDP should sell:

- Feeling
- Ritual
- Emotional state
- Calm productivity
- Identity
- Lifestyle
- Product confidence

The PDP should not lead with:

- Ingredients first
- Biohacker performance claims
- Supplement-stack language
- Dense education
- Too many icons
- Medicalized claims

## Ideal PDP Order

1. **Hero / Purchase**
   - Lead with coffee ritual and emotional promise.
   - Show product/lifestyle imagery clearly.
   - Purchase panel should be calm, legible, and conversion-focused.
   - Subscription/quantity should feel simple.

2. **Immediate Feeling**
   - Explain the state: calm focus, no jitters, no crash, sleep-friendly.
   - Use a few strong benefit statements, not a crowded icon wall.

3. **Ritual Story**
   - Show how STUNN fits into the day.
   - Morning, afternoon, evening without the caffeine tradeoff.
   - This is the emotional bridge between product and life.

4. **Mechanism**
   - Introduce decaf coffee plus functional ingredients.
   - Ingredients should support the story, not lead it.

5. **Comparison**
   - STUNN vs regular coffee should be clear and restrained.
   - The comparison should reinforce why switching feels obvious.

6. **Proof**
   - Reviews, founder story, guarantee, checkout confidence.
   - Human proof should feel editorial, not fake social proof.

7. **FAQ**
   - Remove final friction: prep, timing, box duration, subscription/cancel, caffeine.

## Purchase Panel Philosophy

The purchase panel is the conversion engine, but it should not feel like a supplement funnel.

It should communicate:

- What am I buying?
- How many boxes/months?
- What do I save?
- Can I cancel?
- When does it ship?
- Is checkout safe?

The current strategic product model should be:

- One Shopify product/variant = one box.
- Multi-month options are quantity choices, not separate products.
- Discounts should be handled by Shopify automatic discounts where possible.
- Cart and PDP should explain quantity in plain language.

## Hero Guidance

The hero should not be a claim dump.

Good hero:

- One strong image.
- One strong emotional line.
- One short supporting paragraph.
- Purchase path close by.
- Minimal badges.

Avoid:

- Floating benefit cards that feel pasted on.
- Too many product thumbnails above the fold.
- Loud icons.
- Claims that compete with the headline.

## Ingredient Guidance

Ingredients are important, but they are not the top-level desire.

Ingredient sections should:

- Look like premium editorial product education.
- Use real ingredient photography only if quality is strong.
- Explain why the ingredient improves the coffee ritual.
- Avoid sounding like a clinical supplement label.

Recommended framing:

- Lion's Mane: clarity
- Rhodiola: resilience
- Cordyceps: steady energy
- L-Theanine: calm alertness

## Conversion Guidance

Conversion flow should be calm and obvious:

- Primary CTA leads to purchase panel.
- Cart should reinforce value with quantity-based tiers.
- Free shipping progress should use STUNN light purple, not generic green.
- Checkout CTA should be visually dominant.
- Subscription explanation should be concise and reassuring.

## Current PDP Weaknesses

Observed in current code:

- Product purchase still appears duration/variant based in the PDP panel, while the cart strategy has moved toward quantity-based boxes.
- The hero contains benefit pills and multiple product proof images, which can feel more DTC/supplement than luxury ritual.
- The benefits grid is more controlled than before, but still risks feeling like UI if overused.
- The timeline section uses available imagery, but the images are infographic-style rather than cinematic ritual photography.
- Ingredients/comparison are combined into a dense content gallery. Useful, but currently more functional than premium.

## Redesign Approach

Do not redesign everything at once.

Improve in this order:

1. Align PDP purchase strategy with one-box quantity model.
2. Refine hero hierarchy and image/copy balance.
3. Reduce above-fold clutter.
4. Make benefit section more editorial and less icon-led.
5. Upgrade mechanism/ingredients section into premium product education.
6. Tighten comparison and proof.
7. Re-audit mobile spacing and cart flow.
